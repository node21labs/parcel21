NIP-XX
======

RGB Consignment Exchange over Nostr
-----------------------------------

`draft` `optional`

> **Status:** working draft (Parcel21, Node21 Labs). `NIP-XX` and the inner-rumor kind numbers
> (`1517`/`1518`/`1519`) and the `rgbnostr:` transport scheme are **provisional**, pending NIP-number
> assignment and registration of the transport type upstream in RGB (`RGB-WG/rgb-std`). They are
> derived from a verified review of NIP-01/11/40/44/59 and the RGB HTTP JSON-RPC v0.2 protocol, and an
> adversarial security review whose findings are encoded as the **MUST** requirements below.

## Abstract

This NIP defines how [RGB](https://rgb.tech) **consignments** — the client-side-validated data that
completes an RGB asset transfer — are exchanged over Nostr. It is a decentralized, metadata-private
replacement for the centralized [RGB HTTP JSON-RPC](https://github.com/RGB-Tools/rgb-http-json-rpc) proxy
model. A sender delivers a consignment to a receiver as a [NIP-44]-encrypted, [NIP-59] gift-wrapped
event; the receiver validates it and returns an encrypted acknowledgment to an anonymous reply mailbox.
Relays learn neither consignment contents nor which parties are transacting.

## Motivation

Completing an RGB transfer requires the sender to hand the receiver a **consignment** out of band. The
prevailing mechanism, [RGB HTTP JSON-RPC v0.2](https://github.com/RGB-Tools/rgb-http-json-rpc), is served
by a few publicly operated **proxy servers**, which centralizes a system designed to be
client-side-validated: the operator sees contents and metadata in plaintext, receivers must **poll**,
and availability/censorship-resistance depend on a handful of instances.

Nostr already provides the needed primitives — end-to-end encryption ([NIP-44]), metadata-hiding gift
wrap ([NIP-59]), real-time subscriptions, and a permissionless relay network. This NIP applies them to
RGB consignment exchange while **preserving the exact semantics** wallets already depend on.

## Terminology

- **Consignment** — the RGB transfer data the sender delivers to the receiver for validation.
- **Payer / sender** — transfers the asset; produces and delivers the consignment.
- **Payee / receiver** — receives the asset; issues the invoice, validates the consignment, returns ACK/NACK.
- **`recipient_id`** — the consignment key, equal to the **blinded UTXO** (beneficiary seal) carried in
  the RGB invoice. Generated by the payee and **present in the invoice**, therefore a **public** value
  known to both parties and to anyone who sees the invoice. It reveals no on-chain identity.
- **`attachment_id`** — a content hash keying a media file, decoupled from `recipient_id`.
- **ACK / NACK** — the payee's signal that a *validated* consignment is accepted (ACK) or rejected (NACK).
- **Gift wrap** — the [NIP-59] three-layer construction (rumor → seal → wrap) hiding both content and the
  identities of the communicating parties from relays.
- **Reply key `R`** — a per-transfer **ephemeral** keypair the payer generates; `R`'s public key is the
  anonymous mailbox to which the payee delivers the ACK.

## Roles and keys

| Key | Held by | Lifetime | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Payee receiving key | Payee | dedicated; MAY rotate per invoice | Advertised in the invoice; addresses consignments; signs ACK seals. A dedicated RGB key — **not** the operator's social identity (see [Key management and storage](#key-management-and-storage)) |
| Payer seal key | Payer | **per transfer (ephemeral)** | Signs the consignment seal (`kind 13`). MUST be fresh per transfer (see [Privacy](#privacy-considerations)) |
| Wrap key `W` | Payer & Payee | **per event (one-time)** | Signs each `kind 1059` gift wrap; random, never reused — this is what hides the sender from relays |
| Reply key `R` | Payer | **per transfer (ephemeral)** | Its pubkey is the anonymous ACK mailbox; the only payer-side identifier on the wire, and unlinkable to the payer |

These are all *delivery* keys — they receive and decrypt messages but control no assets; they SHOULD
be derived from the wallet seed and kept separate from custody keys (see
[Key management and storage](#key-management-and-storage)). Ephemeral keys (`W`, `R`, the payer seal
key) are generated per transfer and discarded after settlement.

## Overview of the flow

This NIP maps one-to-one onto the RGB HTTP JSON-RPC v0.2 workflow it replaces:

```
Payee                          Relay(s)                          Payer
  │ (1) invoice: recipient_id + endpoints=rgbnostr:<payee npub>?relay=wss://…
  │ ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────▶
  │                                  (2) build consignment; gen seal key, W, R
  │                  (3) kind:1059 gift wrap → p=payee npub                │
  │ ◀──────────── publish to PAYEE'S relays ◀─────────────────────────────┤
  │ (4) subscribe #p=payee npub, unwrap, VERIFY, RGB-validate              │
  │                  (5) kind:1059 gift wrap → p=R.pub                     │
  ├────────────── publish to PAYEE'S relays ─────────────────────────────▶│
  │                                  (6) subscribe #p=R.pub, unwrap, VERIFY
  │                                  (7) on ACK → broadcast Bitcoin tx
```

All four messages cross the **payee's relays** (the only relay set both parties share — the payer is
anonymous and advertises none). See [§ ACK return path](#the-ack-return-path-anonymous-payer).

## 1. Encryption ([NIP-44])

All encryption is **[NIP-44] v2** (version byte `0x02`): secp256k1 ECDH x-coordinate →
`HKDF-extract(salt = "nip44-v2")` for the conversation key; per-message random 32-byte nonce →
`HKDF-expand` → ChaCha20 + HMAC-SHA256; payload `base64(0x02 ‖ nonce(32) ‖ ciphertext ‖ mac(32))`.
Plaintext is **1–65535 bytes** (hard limit at *each* layer); base64 payload 132–87472 chars.

NIP-44 v2 (Cure53-audited, Dec 2023) provides **no** forward secrecy, post-compromise security, or
post-quantum resistance. This is acceptable for one-shot consignment delivery and is documented as a
limitation.

## 2. Gift wrapping ([NIP-59])

Each Parcel21 message is a [NIP-59] gift wrap built in three layers:

1. **Rumor** — an **unsigned** event (`id` computed, **no `sig`**) of a Parcel21 kind (§4). Carries the
   payload as JSON in `content`.
2. **Seal** — `kind 13`, `content` = NIP-44(rumor) under `conv(sender_seal_priv, recipient_pub)`,
   **signed by the sender's seal key**, `tags` MUST be empty, `created_at` randomized up to 2 days in the
   past.
3. **Gift wrap** — `kind 1059`, `content` = NIP-44(seal) under `conv(W_priv, recipient_pub)`, **signed by
   the one-time wrap key `W`**, `tags = [["p", recipient_pub]]`, `created_at` randomized up to 2 days in
   the past.

Reusing the standard `kind 1059`/`kind 13` means off-the-shelf [NIP-59] tooling interoperates.

### 2.1 Verified unwrap (**MUST**)

> **Security-critical.** [NIP-59] and common libraries (e.g. `nostr-tools` `unwrapEvent`, which performs
> *only* two NIP-44 decryptions) do **not** authenticate the result. Because the rumor is unsigned, an
> attacker who can address a gift wrap to a recipient could otherwise forge a consignment — or forge an
> **ACK** to the payer's reply mailbox and trigger a Bitcoin broadcast.

On receiving any `kind 1059`, a client **MUST**, before acting on the payload:

1. NIP-44-decrypt the wrap with the recipient key → seal; NIP-44-decrypt the seal → rumor.
2. **Verify the seal's Schnorr signature** is valid under `seal.pubkey` (`kind 13`).
3. Assert `seal.pubkey == rumor.pubkey`.
4. Recompute `rumor.id == getEventHash(rumor)`.
5. Assert `rumor.pubkey` equals the **expected counterparty** for this context (the invoice payee npub
   when receiving an ACK; for an inbound consignment, record `rumor.pubkey` and bind subsequent messages
   of the same transfer to it).

A client **MUST** discard any gift wrap failing steps 2–5.

## 3. Discovery

- The payee advertises its relays inline in the invoice (§5) and **MAY** also publish a
  [NIP-17]/[NIP-51] **`kind 10050`** DM-relay-list as a fallback relay source.
- A party offering large consignments/media via Blossom **MAY** publish a **`kind 10063`** Blossom server
  list ([BUD-03]) so recipients can fetch a ciphertext blob across mirrors.

## 4. Event kinds

| Kind | Class | Signed by | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| `1059` gift wrap | regular (stored) | one-time `W` | **The only kind a Parcel21 relay stores / clients subscribe to.** Outer envelope for every message |
| `13` seal | (never published alone) | sender seal key | NIP-44(rumor); authenticates the true author after unwrap |
| `1517` consignment rumor `‹provisional›` | regular | — (unsigned) | `consignment.post`/`get` payload |
| `1518` ack rumor `‹provisional›` | regular | — (unsigned) | `ack.post`/`get` payload |
| `1519` media rumor `‹provisional›` | regular | — (unsigned) | `media.post`/`get` payload |
| `10050` | replaceable | payee | (reused) DM-relay-list for relay discovery |
| `10063` | replaceable | uploader | (reused) Blossom server list ([BUD-03]) |
| `24242` | ephemeral | uploader | (reused) Blossom HTTP authorization ([BUD-01]/[BUD-02]); never sent to a relay |

The inner rumor kinds only ever appear **encrypted inside a seal**, so a relay never indexes them; the
number is a client-side dispatch discriminator. They sit in the regular range and avoid the NIP-17
`14`/`15` and other well-known kinds. **Provisional** pending NIP assignment.

### 4.1 Rumor payloads

**Consignment (`kind 1517`)** — `content` is JSON:

```jsonc
{
  "recipient_id": "txob1nt9ee42…",   // blinded UTXO from the invoice (the rendezvous key)
  "txid": "9ccf8491…",                // witness txid of the transfer (binding)
  "vout": 0,                          // optional
  "sha256": "<hex>",                  // sha256 of the CLEARTEXT consignment bytes (immutability anchor)
  "reply_to": "<hex pubkey of R>",    // anonymous ACK mailbox
  "mode": "inline",                   // "inline" | "blossom"
  "consignment_b64": "<base64>",      // inline mode only
  "blob": {                           // blossom mode only
    "servers": ["https://blossom.example"],
    "sha256": "<hex of CIPHERTEXT>",  // content address of the encrypted blob
    "size": 1048576,
    "encryption": { "algo": "nip44" } // see §6
  }
}
```

**ACK/NACK (`kind 1518`)** — `content` is JSON:

```jsonc
{
  "recipient_id": "txob1nt9ee42…",
  "ack": true,                        // true = ACK, false = NACK
  "consignment_sha256": "<hex>",      // binds the ACK to the exact consignment it answers
  "reason": "…"                       // optional, for NACK
}
```

**Media (`kind 1519`)** — like the consignment rumor but keyed by `attachment_id` instead of
`recipient_id`/`txid`.

## 5. Invoice transport endpoint

RGB invoices carry an `endpoints=` list (e.g. `rpc://`, `rpcs://`, or the RGB-Tools `rgbhttpjsonrpc:`
scheme mapping to `TransportType::JsonRpc`). This NIP defines a new transport type:

```
rgbnostr:<payee-npub>?relay=wss://relay.parcel21.example[&relay=wss://backup.example]
```

- `<payee-npub>` — the payee key to address consignments to (and to verify ACK seals against).
- one or more `relay=` parameters — the relays the payer publishes to **and** subscribes to for the ACK.

Registration of the `rgbnostr` `TransportType` upstream (`RGB-WG/rgb-std`, `rgb-lib`) is tracked as
provisional. Until then, Parcel21-aware wallets parse it from the same `endpoints=` list.

## 6. Storage of consignment data

Two content-addressed tiers:

- **Inline.** If the serialized rumor stays safely under the NIP-44 per-layer plaintext limit (65535
  bytes at *each* of the two layers), the consignment is base64-encoded directly into the rumor. Because
  base64 inflates by ~33 % and is wrapped twice, the inline budget **MUST** be computed against the
  **final serialized rumor `content` length** and asserted `< 65535` before sealing. A safe raw-payload
  cap is **~32–40 KB** (base64 ≈ 43–54 KB plus JSON envelope). *(Note: a naïve "48 KB raw" cap fails —
  `base64(48 KiB) = 65536 > 65535`.)*
- **External ([Blossom]).** Otherwise — the default for real consignments (tens of KB to several MB) —
  the payer **NIP-44-encrypts the consignment first**, uploads only the **ciphertext** blob via
  [BUD-02] `PUT /upload` (content-addressed by the ciphertext sha256, authorized by a fresh short-expiry
  `kind 24242` token, `t=upload`), and references `{servers, sha256, size, encryption}` inside the
  gift-wrapped rumor. Mirror to ≥2 servers from the uploader's `kind 10063` list. The recipient **MUST**
  recompute and compare the ciphertext sha256 **before** decrypting (integrity check against a malicious
  or buggy server), then NIP-44-decrypts. Neither the relay (sees a `kind 1059` to a random key) nor the
  Blossom server (sees opaque ciphertext) learns plaintext or the parties.

Blob encryption key: use a **per-blob random symmetric key wrapped to the recipient inside the rumor**
(not a key derived from a long-lived pairwise relationship), consistent with the per-transfer ephemeral
payer seal key.

## 7. Method mapping

| RGB HTTP JSON-RPC v0.2 | Parcel21 over Nostr |
|---|---|
| `server.info` | Relay [NIP-11] document (`software`, `version`, `supported_nips`, `limitation`, a `parcel21` marker). Optional live ping = ephemeral `kind 21059` |
| `consignment.post` | Payer publishes `kind 1059` (seal `kind 13` over unsigned `kind 1517`) `p`-tagged to the payee npub, to the payee's relays. Idempotency → receiver first-**validated**-wins (§8) |
| `consignment.get` | Payee `REQ {kinds:[1059], "#p":[payee_pub], since:…}`, verified-unwraps each (§2.1) to a `kind 1517` rumor; reads inline or fetches the Blossom blob. `-400` ≙ no matching gift wrap |
| `media.post` / `media.get` | Same as consignment, over `kind 1519`, keyed by `attachment_id`. `-401` ≙ no media rumor/blob |
| `ack.post` | Payee publishes `kind 1059` (seal `kind 13` over unsigned `kind 1518`) `p`-tagged to `R.pub`, **to the payee's relays**. Write-once enforced locally (§8) |
| `ack.get` | Payer `REQ {kinds:[1059], "#p":[R.pub]}`, verified-unwraps to `kind 1518`. **Subscription, not polling.** Tri-state: `ack=true`→ACK, `ack=false`→NACK, no rumor→pending |

## 8. The ACK return path (anonymous payer)

In the proxy model the payer is anonymous and **polls** `ack.get`. Over Nostr there is no server to poll;
the payee must **deliver** the ACK. Parcel21 keeps the payer anonymous:

1. The payer generates a fresh **reply key `R`** per transfer and places `R.pub` **inside the encrypted
   consignment rumor** (`reply_to`). `R.pub` never appears in cleartext on the wire.
2. The payee gift-wraps its ACK/NACK to `R.pub`.
3. **Relay choice (MUST):** because the payer advertises no relays of its own, the ACK is published to
   the **payee's invoice relays** — the only relay set both parties share. The payer subscribes for
   `{kinds:[1059], "#p":[R.pub]}` on those same relays.
4. The payer applies [verified unwrap](#21-verified-unwrap-must), asserts the seal author equals the
   invoice payee, and asserts `consignment_sha256` matches what it sent.

**Offline payer:** the gift wrap is a stored (`kind 1059`) event, so an offline payer reconnects and
re-`REQ`s with `since`. Senders **SHOULD** attach a [NIP-40] `expiration` no earlier than the RGB
invoice expiry so relays do not prune an ACK before the payer returns.

## 9. Immutability, replay, and binding (**MUST**)

The proxy enforced two write-once invariants via a single trusted store. An open, multi-relay set cannot;
Parcel21 reconstructs them at the **receiver**, content-addressed, and **gated on RGB validation** so a
public `recipient_id` cannot be griefed:

- **Consignment — first *validated* wins.** Since `recipient_id` is public (it is in the invoice), any
  observer could publish a competing consignment for it. The payee therefore **MUST NOT** pin on arrival
  order alone. It pins the first consignment whose bytes **successfully RGB-validate against the awaited
  transfer** (`txid`/UTXO), and **MUST** keep accepting further submissions for the same `recipient_id`
  until one validates (rather than locking onto a junk-first event). An identical `sha256` re-submission
  is a no-op (proxy `false`); a *different* validated consignment after one is pinned is rejected (proxy
  `-101`).
- **ACK — first valid wins.** The payee emits at most one ACK/NACK per `recipient_id` and **MUST NOT**
  emit the opposite later (proxy `-100`). The payer treats the **first** gift wrap that passes
  [verified unwrap](#21-verified-unwrap-must), is authored by the invoice payee, and whose
  `consignment_sha256` matches, as authoritative; later flips are ignored.
- **Transfer binding.** The payee binds a consignment to the awaited transfer via the RGB validation
  above, **not** by trusting the rumor's `txid` string.

A malicious relay can **withhold or reorder** gift wraps but cannot **forge** content without breaking
the sha256 / seal signature. Recipients **SHOULD** read from multiple relays; "first-seen" is relative to
the recipient's relay view.

## 10. Relay behavior

A Parcel21 relay:

- **MUST** accept `kind 1059` (and **MAY** accept ephemeral `kind 21059` for the optional ping) and
  **SHOULD** reject other kinds.
- **MUST** advertise its raised `max_message_length` / `max_content_length` and a `parcel21` marker in
  its [NIP-11] `limitation` object, so clients detect capacity before publishing.
- **SHOULD** retain stored gift wraps for async pickup and honor [NIP-40] `expiration` for pruning.
- **Anti-spam — pick one lane** (gift wraps carry random one-time keys, so pubkey bans are impossible):
  either an **open** relay protected by [NIP-13] PoW + payment + per-connection byte budgets, **or** a
  **[NIP-42] AUTH-gated** relay for known counterparties (which forgoes payer anonymity *toward the relay
  operator*). A relay **MUST NOT** advertise AUTH-gating as compatible with anonymous payers.

## 11. Error semantics

The proxy's JSON-RPC error space collapses, relay-less, to local outcomes: `-101`/`-100` → the
immutability rules of §9; `-400`/`-401` → "no matching gift wrap/blob on the relay(s)"; malformed or
unverifiable payloads → discarded per §2.1.

## Key management and storage

The Nostr keys in this protocol are **delivery keys, not custody keys** — they receive and decrypt
consignment/ACK messages but control no assets. Custody stays entirely with the wallet's Bitcoin/RGB
signing keys. That separation drives the storage model.

- **Derive; don't store a new secret.** A client SHOULD derive the payee receiving key — and the
  per-transfer `R`, seal, and `W` keys — deterministically from the wallet seed on a dedicated path
  (one account for the receiving key; per-transfer keys at successive indices). Every key is then
  recoverable from the seed the user already backs up, and the wallet need only persist *non-secret*
  state (which indices have in-flight transfers). Clients MUST NOT require a separate backup of Nostr
  key material.
- **Dedicated, not social.** The payee receiving key SHOULD be dedicated to RGB and distinct from the
  operator's social identity, and MAY rotate per invoice. Because it is advertised out-of-band in the
  invoice rather than discovered by a social `npub`, a dedicated — ideally per-invoice — key maximizes
  unlinkability (cf. on-chain Silent Payments, [BIP-352]): an observer sees consignments addressed to
  unrelated one-time keys, with no link to a persistent identity.
- **Hot vs. cold.** Decrypting an inbound gift wrap needs the receiving key's secret at receive time
  ([NIP-44] ECDH), so it is necessarily a "hot" key. This is acceptable precisely because it is
  delivery-only and value-less; custody keys SHOULD remain separate and MAY be kept cold or in
  hardware. A client MAY instead delegate decryption to a [NIP-07]/[NIP-46] signer that performs
  `nip44` decryption without exposing the secret.
- **Loss and rotation lose no funds.** For future transfers the payee advertises a freshly derived
  key. For a consignment in flight to a lost key, the payer still holds it and can re-deliver it — to
  a new key or any other transport — so recovery is a re-request, not a loss. Restoring the wallet
  seed re-derives all delivery keys.

## Privacy considerations

| Observer | Learns | Does **not** learn |
|---|---|---|
| Relay | Opaque `kind 1059` to random keys; size; timing | Contents, real sender/receiver, `recipient_id`, `txid` |
| Blossom server | Ciphertext blob; downloader IP | Plaintext, parties, `recipient_id` |
| Invoice holder | `recipient_id` (blinded), payee npub, relay URLs | Contents, whether/when delivered |

- The payer's **seal key MUST be fresh per transfer**; otherwise the payee can link the payer's transfers
  by the seal pubkey it sees on unwrap.
- Residual leaks gift wrap does **not** cover: `created_at` (mitigated by ±2-day randomization), client IP
  (use Tor/mixnet; out of scope), and **event size** (NIP-44 padding is coarse — a multi-MB consignment
  wrap is distinguishable from a small ACK wrap, allowing a shared relay to correlate a transfer's two
  legs by size+timing). Routing the consignment via Blossom shrinks the on-relay consignment leg and
  reduces this correlation.
- Do **not** publish a self-copy of the consignment to `R.pub` (NIP-17's self-copy convention targets a
  sender's *identity* key for multi-device sync and does not apply to an identity-less payer; a second
  event to `R.pub` only widens the correlatable footprint). Keep local records locally.
